# Nanoscale Thermometry in Biological Systems

## Background

Accurate temperature measurement at the subcellular level has long been a challenge in biophysics. Recent work on nanoscale thermometry (DOI: 10.1038/nature12373) demonstrates that nitrogen-vacancy centers in diamond nanocrystals can measure local temperature gradients within living cells with sub-degree precision.

The technique exploits the temperature dependence of spin resonance frequencies, achieving spatial resolution below 200 nm. See also https://www.nature.com/articles/nature12373 for the full publisher page and supplementary materials.

## Open Questions

Whether these measurements reflect true intracellular temperature heterogeneity or are confounded by local chemical environment changes remains debated. The proposed method reduces variance and improves calibration compared to earlier fluorescence-based approaches, though systematic benchmarking against established calorimetric techniques is still lacking.

Several groups have since extended this approach to in vivo measurements in model organisms, but the relationship between nanoscale thermal gradients and cellular metabolic activity requires further investigation.
