# Reader Profile

**Paper:** SciRAG — Retrieval-Augmented Generation for Automated Scientific Literature Review

**Who they are:** NLP / information retrieval researcher familiar with language models and retrieval systems, but not specializing in scientometrics or library science.

**Background they reliably have:**
- Transformer-based language models (GPT, BERT family) and their training/inference pipelines
- Dense passage retrieval (DPR, ColBERT) and approximate nearest-neighbor search (FAISS, ScaNN)
- Retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) as a concept and its use in open-domain QA
- Standard NLP evaluation metrics (BLEU, ROUGE, BERTScore, factual consistency metrics)
- Basic familiarity with systematic literature reviews as a research method

**Background they do NOT reliably have:**
- Scientometrics terminology (h-index distributions, bibliometric coupling, co-citation analysis)
- Domain-specific knowledge in biology or materials science (the non-CS evaluation domains)
- Library science standards for systematic reviews (PRISMA, Cochrane protocols)
